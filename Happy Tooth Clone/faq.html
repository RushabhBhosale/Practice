FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Ask Away
Why choose a pediatric dentist?

Dr. Hanks completed an additional two years of training (beyond the 8 years of school required to become a dentist) at a Childrens Hospital where he became certified in Pediatric Advanced Life Support, a hospital-certified Spanish interpreter, and a specialist in childrens dentistry.

When should I bring my child in for a first visit?

Dr. Hanks follows the AAPD guideline to establish a dental home for children at first tooth or first birthday, whichever comes first.

How long has Dr. Hanks been a dentist?

Dr. Hanks started as a pediatric dental assistant in 2010 and has been training to become or working as a licensed dentist ever since.

What does a Happy Start mean?

Dr. Hanks has put thought into every detail of his practice to ensure every child starts their dental journey on the right foot! Dr. Hanks works closely with parents to make every visit comfortable for each child.

What is an MPH?

Dr. Hanks earned both a Doctorate in Medical Dentistry and a Masters in Public health during his four years of dental school. Dr. Hanks earned an MPH to be able to understand and effectively treat the mouth behind the tooth, the child behind the mouth, the family behind the child and the community behind the family.

What types of insurances do you accept?

We accept every type of insurance offered in the area including Medicaid.

What languages are spoke in the office?

English, Spanish and ASL.
